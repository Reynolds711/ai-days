Core idea

Context + constraints = bounded agency

Agents become useful when they have enough context to understand the work and enough constraints to act safely.

Context Requests, policies, documents, history, systems
+
Constraints Permissions, approvals, data boundaries, escalation rules
=
Bounded agency Summarize, route, draft, recommend, prepare, trigger
The goal is not unlimited autonomy. The goal is bounded agency inside a workflow.
Four phases

Move from idea to pilot path.

These phases mirror the workshop sprints: navigate the problem, bound the handoff, map the platform path, and declare the next step.

1

Pick the workflow pain

Start with the process where work gets stuck, repeated, routed, delayed, or re-explained.

  • Who is waiting?
  • Where does the handoff break?
  • What would improve in 30 days?
2

Design the handoff

Define the route, role, and rules before choosing a tool or building an agent.

  • What starts the workflow?
  • What should the agent prepare?
  • Where does a person approve?
3

Map the platform path

Choose the smallest practical starting point and the path to mature safely over time.

  • Explore with Copilot Chat
  • Scale team workflows with agents
  • Connect systems with Power Platform, Graph, Fabric, or Azure AI
4

Declare the pilot

Leave with an owner, a pilot group, a data boundary, and a success measure.

  • Who owns the workflow?
  • What is the smallest useful version?
  • What follow-up is needed?
Workflow lanes

Choose the lane closest to the work.

Do not worry about finding a perfect organizational match. Choose the lane where the workflow pain is clearest.

Selected lane

Resident service & casework

Use this lane when the workflow involves requests, eligibility prep, missing information, routing, resident responses, or backlog reduction.

A request, form, email, case, or service ticket arrives.
Summarize the request, identify missing information, recommend routing, and draft the handoff.
Eligibility, enforcement, sensitive decisions, and final responses stay human-approved.
Faster routing, fewer incomplete handoffs, reduced backlog, and more consistent responses.
