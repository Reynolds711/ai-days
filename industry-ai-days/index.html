Your job is a bundle of tasks orchestrated toward an outcome.

Industry AI Days should begin by helping customers see work differently: not as roles, org charts, or apps, but as repeatable tasks, human judgments, system handoffs, and decisions that produce an organizational result.

What customers should notice

Most workflows contain a mix of repeatable work, ambiguous work, compliance boundaries, and relationship moments. AI value starts when those pieces are named instead of treated as one indivisible job.

The reframing question

Which tasks are rule-based enough to delegate, and which moments still require human decision, nuance, judgment, accountability, or gray-area interpretation?

Delegation in the AI era is not autonomy. It is bounded agency.

The agent becomes useful when it has enough context to understand the work and enough constraints to act safely inside the workflow.

Context Requests, policies, knowledge, history, records, owners, systems, and current state.
+
Constraints Permissions, approval gates, escalation rules, data boundaries, risk thresholds, and audit needs.
=
Bounded agency Summarize, classify, route, draft, recommend, prepare, compare, trigger, and escalate.

Not the pitch

"Let an agent run the process."

The better pitch

"Let an agent own the bounded handoff until a human decision is needed."

The workshop move

Build a simple web-based declarative agent spec before debating platforms.

The hard part is not the agent. The hard part is the triage model.

When customers rethink a process around an outcome, they need to be explicit about which tasks are delegated to agents and where the work returns to humans.

Agent-owned task lane

  • Collect and normalize inputs from systems of record.
  • Summarize the case, request, incident, or opportunity.
  • Identify missing information and next-best action.
  • Draft the handoff, response, packet, or recommendation.

Human-owned judgment lane

  • Approve eligibility, risk, exception, enforcement, or spend.
  • Decide where policy is ambiguous or stakes are high.
  • Handle sensitive relationship or customer moments.
  • Own the final accountability for action and outcome.

Every industry session should leave with a curated workflow grounded in systems of record.

The goal is a high-value scenario tied to strategic priorities, not a generic agent demo. Use the selector to pressure-test the same bounded agency model across industry contexts.

Each example is intentionally SOW-shaped: outcome, source systems, delegated tasks, human triage, and pilot measure.

Frontier organizations make rule-based process work feel invisible.

The GPS analogy is the north star: 25 years ago, GPS sat visibly on the dashboard. Now it is ambient in the phone, silently routing decisions and reshaping behavior at societal scale.

From dashboard tool to ambient operating layer.

What becomes invisible

  • Rule-based routing, classification, status checks, and repetitive coordination.
  • Information gathering across documents, records, policies, and systems.
  • Handoff prep that used to depend on memory, email archaeology, and tribal knowledge.
  • Process guidance that keeps humans focused on judgment instead of administration.

The deliverable is one agent workflow customers can actually pilot.

Every customer should leave with a bounded workflow they can bake into a partner or SI statement of work for an immediate proof of concept.

1. Name the outcome

Anchor on a strategic priority: retention, throughput, backlog, safety, revenue, compliance, service quality, or operational resilience.

2. Declare the handoff

Specify the trigger, data sources, delegated task bundle, human decision point, and escalation rules.

3. Shape the SOW

Define source-system access, pilot group, success measure, governance boundary, partner build path, and next checkpoint.

Workshop close

We are not asking customers to imagine "AI everywhere." We are asking them to identify one strategic workflow where bounded agency can reduce friction, increase consistency, and create measurable value.
